# EA Safariroute: Field Representative Toolkit

## 1. Daily Station Checklist (Nairobi, Dar, Kampala)
- **Presence**: Arrive at the station/bus park 30 minutes before major departures (e.g., SGR morning train).
- **Brand Visibility**: Wear the EA Safariroute badge/t-shirt.
- **Support**: Check in with the ticket clerks. Ask: "Any issues with Safariroute codes today?"
- **Onboarding**: Identify travelers looking confused or struggling with queues. Offer to help them book via the app for their next trip.

## 2. The "Elevator Pitch" for Bus Managers
*Use this when visiting offices of companies like Modern Coast, Tahmeed, or Global Coaches.*
> "Hi, I'm [Name] from EA Safariroute. We are bringing you more passengers through our digital platform. We don't handle your cash—travelers pay you directly. We just provide them with a unique tracking code. At the end of the month, we just ask for a 5% commission on the passengers we actually delivered to your seats. It's zero risk for you, and more business for your fleet."

## 3. Problem Resolution (The "Quick Fix")
**Scenario**: Clerk says "I don't know what this code is."
1. **Rep Action**: Politely explain that the code is a lead-tracking ID from your partner platform.
2. **Verification**: Show the clerk the passenger's name and route details on the rep-version of the app.
3. **Escalation**: If the clerk refuses, note the Clerk ID and Station Name. Call the Manager (whom your rep should have on speed dial).

## 4. Weekly Reporting to HQ
Each Friday, the Rep sends a report with:
- **Total Redemptions**: How many codes were successfully used.
- **Operator Feedback**: Which bus lines are happy, and which need more convincing.
- **Niche Routes**: Any new "secret" routes discovered (e.g., a new shuttle line starting up).
